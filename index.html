ABOUT

Octavio Villalobos

I'm a currently persuing a Master's degree in Computer Science at UC Santa Cruz. I also complete my undergade at UCSC in Computer Science. During my time at UCSC, I developed a strong interest in artificial intelligence, specifically in deep learning and embodied AI.

My research centers on embodied AI. I'm interested in how intelligent systems learn internal representations directly from raw sensory data and use those representations to predict future states, reason about complex environments, and interact with them.

Right now I'm developing a reinforcement learning framework for non-player characters (NPCs) that enables more dynamic, responsive behaviors in simulated environments. Through this work, I explore how agent-based systems learn from interactions and develop richer internal models of their worlds. More broadly, I'm interested in building AI systems that move beyond static prediction toward agents that can understand, anticipate, and act within their environments.
